Why Should People Have a License for Having Children?

Raising a child is one of the biggest responsibilities a human can have. This presentation explores whether becoming a parent should require preparation, education, and qualification before bringing a child into the world. Now let’s talk about "How qualified a person should be to be allowed to have children?"

Main question: Why?

First: Pregnancy

How hard it is to carry a child?


How does the baby affect the mother?

Pregnancy is not only a physical process, but also an emotional and psychological journey. Carrying a child requires major sacrifices from the mother and can affect every part of her life. A study published by PubMed revealed the gap women face between reality and expectations. Because Society either portrays pregnancy as an ideal period, while reality is something else or it simply does not talk enough about it. Studies from Harvard and Yale universities have found that pregnancy stress can increase a woman’s biological age by up to two years.

How does the mother affect the baby?

During pregnancy, the baby depends entirely on the mother’s health, nutrition, mental state, and environment. Stress, unhealthy habits, or lack of care can directly affect the child’s development. A Simple example on this The study by Claudia M. Buss in 2012, it shows that there’s a connection between a woman’s higher cortisol levels and how the baby’s brain develops.

This raises an important question: should every person automatically be considered ready for such responsibility?

Second: Birth & Postpartum Depression

Childbirth is physically exhausting and emotionally intense. Many mothers experience postpartum depression (PPD), which can affect their emotional well-being and ability to care for their child.

I asked my mother and a couple of relatives : did you expect the pain of giving birth? the answer was “no, we could never imagine something like this “, most of them commented that no one had told them anything about it , except that it was supposed to be a wonderful experience. This kind of ignorance can lead to postpartum depression PPD.

Hormonal changes after birth can strongly influence the brain and emotions. For example, breastfeeding mothers often experience lower dopamine levels while producing prolactin for milk production.

Without emotional support, education, or preparation, both the parent and child may suffer during this period.
